# Why a personal website?
After creating my first blog, creating an interactive website for myself has always been in the back of my mind.
The catalyst for initiating this project was artist [Chungi Yoo's website](https://www.chungiyoo.com/). The website was so pretty.

## Moving to Figma
I've encountered Figma before, but never quite used it a lot. So, I had to learn the shortcuts and methods by searching on the Internet, per usual. I took a lot of inspiration from other websites, and throughout the process, learned about the so many cool websites there are.

### Font
One of the first things I did was choosing the typography. I wanted to have something playful to display a fun personality. Not sure if this is cohesive (it looks good on my side), but I ended up choosing on 4 fonts in total.

The first one was Aref Ruqaa, which I thought resembled my handwriting the most, so it could show who I am. However, I only used it once in the footnote to write my name (I find the footnote to be pretty). I also decided on Averia Sans Libre, which was a younger-looking and cuter font. I used it mainly on content that I wanted to present in small clumps. Another one that I was initially unsure of was Cormorant Infant, but it was very elegant and suited the titles of the pages. My last one was slowly integrated in, because I thought a more standard-looking font served paragraph content more naturally. Here are some pictures of the fonts:

### Color
I also had to choose colors. I wanted to find a color scheme with one of those color scheme tools online, but it was quite difficult to figure out a good color scheme that I liked and was accessible. At the end, I found the default Figma colors to be cute and full of contrast. Here is a picture of the colors:

The background color is a light cream. I realized I liked using cream and brown a lot (my current blog uses a theme like that), but I wanted to give more openness to the eyes, so I made the cream background lighter.

## Home page
My landing page, to be honest, resembles many portfolio websites. There's my name really big and menu options underneath. I tried making this, as well as everything else, pleasurable to the eyes (at least it does for me, so hopefully to others too haha). Also that cs pun I tried to make lol.

My introduction and journey page took slight inspo from https://ayasasakura.com/, with the layered blocks as background (check this to make sure) and dark background hugging a story. ## About me page
Because I kind of cover all my technical resume stuff in the home page, I thought I'd use the About me page to show my non-cs side. This include less technical clubs I am or was involved in, my interests, and classes at university that I found to be interesting.

The landing page for the About me page took reference the sticky scrolling from https://ayasasakura.com/.

My other clubs page took inspo from https://takeboost.com/. This website has such good engagement and cohesiveness.


## Footnote and Nav bar
Most inspiration for my footnote comes from https://ayasasakura.com/. I changed somethings, such as blending it in my theme, and I really love how it turned out, especially the dynamics between the fonts. 

I'm currently still unsure about the nav bar. I like having a left nav bar, but it's difficult making the page look balanced.
